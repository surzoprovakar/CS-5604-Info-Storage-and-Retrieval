professional education summer and winter sessions experiential learning living-learning programs study abroad theatre arts the theatre arts program provides a comprehensive education in theatre within the context of a broad liberal arts course of study. it is accredited by the national association of schools of theatre. the curriculum emphasizes the theoretical, the historical, and the practical. the program provides a foundation in dramatic literature, theatre and film history, play-writing, and production. advanced courses are available in acting, scene and costume design, lighting, theatre production, and cinema studies and production. the department produces between 12 and 18 theatre pieces annually, led by practicing artists/teachers. students are encouraged to become involved in all areas of production. auditions and crew assignments are open to all university students. career options performer, designer, technician, or director for professional theatre companies and
